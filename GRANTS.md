# Alternative License Grants — Public Record

This page is the public record of **alternative license grants** issued by
the `DynBufReader` maintainer under the template in
[ALTERNATIVE-LICENSE.md](ALTERNATIVE-LICENSE.md).

It is informational, not authoritative. The signed agreement with each
Licensee is what actually controls that Licensee's rights. The list here
exists so that anyone reading the project can see, at a glance, who has been
given exceptions to the public `AGPL-3.0-only` terms, what kind of work those
exceptions cover, and how many quietly exist that are not listed by name.

## How listings work

Each grant is assigned a unique, sequential **grant number** when it is
issued. Grant numbers are permanent: they do not get reused, and a grant
keeps the same number when it moves from **Active grants** to **Expired
grants**, or when a Licensee changes their listing preference.

Numbering is sequential across all grants, listed or not. This means gaps
will appear in the public numbering where unlisted grants sit — that is
expected, and the total count of those gaps always matches the
**Undisclosed count** below.

The Maintainer keeps a private record that maps grant numbers to the
actual Licensee identity and signed-agreement details for every grant,
listed or not. That private record is the authoritative source; this
public page is a view of it filtered by each Licensee's listing
preference.

Each Licensee chooses, when requesting a grant, how they want to appear in
this record. The options are:

- **Listed by name** — the Licensee and the Approved Use are named directly
  under the grant number, optionally with a link.
- **Listed by category** — the grant appears under its number with a
  generic label, for example *"A non-profit organization"* or *"A commercial
  company (undisclosed)"*, without naming the entity or the Approved Use.
- **Not listed** — the grant does not appear as an entry at all. It is
  still assigned a grant number (which may show up as a gap in the public
  numbering) and is counted in the **Undisclosed count** below.

The default, if the Licensee expresses no preference, is **listed by
category**. Licensees may change their preference at any time by writing to
the Maintainer; the grant number stays the same across changes.

Grants that end — through change-of-control decline, breach termination,
mutual wind-down, or Licensee request — are moved from **Active grants** to
**Expired grants**, not removed. History is part of the record.

## Undisclosed count

- **Active, not listed by name or category:** 0
- **Expired, not listed by name or category:** 0

These counts, together with the visible entries below, should account for
every grant number ever issued. In other words: the highest grant number
that appears below, plus any higher grant numbers the Maintainer has
issued but not shown here, should equal the total of all visible entries
plus the two counts above. Gaps in the public numbering correspond to
grants sitting in these counts. If the numbers do not reconcile, this
file is out of date — please open an issue.
